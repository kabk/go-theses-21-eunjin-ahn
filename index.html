Intolerable Monstrous Instinct

Jennifer of Jennifer‚Äôs Body(2009)

Usually monsters are expressed as beings with masculine characteristics, and female monsters are significantly less than male monsters. Therefore, the discourse around horror films has always been the composition of male monsters versus female victims. Monsters were mostly male and women were the objects that monsters wanted to take, and they existed as rewards for men when they fought monsters. The male monsters in Hollywood horror films have been reproduced as werewolves, vampires, zombies, apes, monsters, and cannibals. They reproduces the fear of the weak and vulnerable in society and the dark and bizarre side of our unconscious nature. On the other hand, female monsters in Western films appear as mothers, witches, vampires and castration. The female monsters in the patriarchal and phallic world are closely related to the problems of sex difference and castration. The Exorcist(1973), the main character of a possessed girl, Carrie(1976), featuring a witchy mother and a daughter who became a monster showed female monsters with a strong presence as ‚Äòmonsters‚Äô, but their femininity and monstrous nature were not closely analyzed together for a long time.

In The Monstrous Feminine Barbara Creed explains five types of female monsters (the archaic mother, the monstrous womb, the witch, the vampire and the possessed women) along with three features of female monsters in relation to Julia Kristeva‚Äôs theory of the abject and motherhood.19 In Kristeva‚Äôs essay, the abject means what disturbs identity, system and order. Hollywood's horror film depicts abjection in at least three ways. First, horror films are overflowing with filthy images of the abject such as shit, blood, urine, pus and the bodies, which are mixed with the flesh. Second, monsters in horror films break down and threaten all boundaries, including human and inhuman, man and beast, normal and the supernatural, good and evil, and the border of gender roles. Thirdly, Horror films constitute the maternal characteristics of women as the abject. Creed quotes Kristeva and criticizes Freud to analyzes the female image in the horror film. Freud said that humans feel fear from ‚Äòthe return of the repressed‚Äô. This can be interpreted as ‚Äòuncanny‚Äô defined by him. Freud explains that when the otherness, which was socially oppressed returns, humans feel bizarre and this leads to fear. If the original familiarity is oppressed, the object becomes ‚Äòthe other‚Äô and becomes the unfamiliar one. Something oppressed always struggles to return, and this unfamiliar thing that has returned becomes the other and gives fear to humans. Freud claims that women are threatening because they are castrated, and assumes female monsters as images of ‚Äòcastrating woman‚Äô and ‚Äòpallic mother.‚Äô Creed writes:
‚ÄúRepresentations of woman as an agent of castration take various forms in the horror film: oral sadistic mother (the vampire film); femme castratrice (the woman‚Äôs revenge film); castrating mother (family horror). Recurring images and motifs associated with woman as castrator include knives, axes, ice picks, spiked instruments, teeth, yawning chasms, jagged rocks, the deadly vagina dentata. In comparison with other genres, the horror genre does not attempt to soothe castration anxiety.‚Äù20
Looking back at the images of female monsters on the screen, Creed refutes Freud‚Äôs old declaration. Are you afraid of women because they are castrated, or are you afraid of being castrated by them?



Carrie of Carrie(1976)

Carrie(1976) is a representative work that does not fall out of the subject of female monsters and connects the life characteristics and fear of women, such as menarche, rites of passage, primitiveness and motherhood. There are controls, fears, and punishments that show patriarchal order around the menstruation and sexuality of adolescent women. In Carrie, Carrie is a girl who suffers from adolescence and is portrayed as a supernatural being, not ‚Äònormal‚Äô. She is forced to be chastised by her morbidly devout mother and bullied by everyone in school. One day she had her first period at the school and teased by her classmate because she has not been properly educated about it. After menarche, the superpowers that were latent to her begin to emerge: ability to move objects freely without touching them. Carrie‚Äôs mother tries to suppress the superpowers that come down to the household for generations more intensely, thinking of them as the ‚Äòpower of the devil.‚Äô But Carrie decides to escape her mother‚Äôs oppression and begins to communicate with the world. Carrie, who was obedient and introverted to her mother, finally overpowers her mother with her psychic powers, and attends a prom party in her own dress. However, the group that bothered Carrie manipulated Carrie to be elected prom queen and poured pig blood when she was at the best moment on stage. This blood means shame and insult, and at the same time it is an opportunity to awaken Carrie's supernatural power. With this blood, Carrie's revenge begins. This film is the best example of the concept of ‚Äòreturned the other‚Äô mentioned above. The miserable being who was suppressed and harassed returns to the monster and gives fear to everyone. Carrie, who became a monster, expresses the anger that has been suppressed to the students and mothers of the school, becomes a monster and finally be honest and free with her own desires.



Justine of Raw(2016)

The similar type of modern female horror film is Raw(2016). Raw is a genre that touches the tradition of horror films such as Carrie and Ginger Snaps(2000). These horror films define the sexuality of a teenage girl as dangerous and abnormal, and the metaphor makes the main character perceive a superpower or a heterogeneous identity. Raw takes Carrie‚Äôs formula but goes to the most radical way without repeating the clich√©. Growing up under strict vegetarian parents, Justine enters the famous veterinary school where her sister Alexia attends. The veterinary department she experienced is far from respecting animals, and is full of masculine, highly hierarchical cultures. Women are subject to sexual harassment, and gay men are subject to ridicule. After a harsh freshman ceremony in which Justine had to force herself to eat animal intestines, she feels an intense desire for raw meat, which she has never felt in her life. Justine feels a sense of satisfaction that she has never experienced before by eating raw meat. One day, while she was being waxed by her sister, Justine accidentally cut off one of her sister‚Äôs finger and while waiting for an ambulance, she even failed to overcome her desire and eats that finger. After this terrible accident, Justine goes through a radical shift from abstinence to cannibalism. The changes Justin experiences when she becomes a freshman are so radical and extreme. The sexual desire and deviation that started to run through brutal rites of passage, and the blood-stained cannibalism that symbolizes it, are shocking, but offer the audience some sense of excitement and joy. Young women, who are consumed only as sexual ‚Äòbody‚Äô by men, subversively eat the ‚Äòbody‚Äô of men and take the position of predators instead of men. The film rejects the boundaries and taboos between the human and the inhuman and shows the abject like rashes, hair, raw meat, biting, and vomiting, and questions about a woman's body. In the end of the film, when Justine finds out that her cannibal desires come from her motherhood, she faces ethical choices. Should I live as a strict vegetarian like my mother, or will I be judged by society by releasing my desires like my sister?

Many female monsters were revealing the position of women who could not show up or speak properly unless they were in horror movies. It is a place where the fear shared in this society become visible and the suppressed minority returns to the monster. The simple and obvious basic formula of horror movies is that ‚Äònormality is threatened by monsters‚Äô. Strange beings threaten normality, and beings that disturb normality become monsters. The exclusions from the everyday space return when the space becomes a non-daily space. Therefore one of the important things in the horror genre is the stage and the background. This is because the horror genre reflects our society and culture. The most absurd place in society is the place of horror.


The Woman of The Woman(2011)

The Woman(2011) shows wild female monster that confront civilization in the background of a harsh patriarchal family. In The Woman, the space of horror is a home in which a violent, psychopath father wields enormous power within his family. The lawyer who happened to witness the last wild cannibal woman (the woman) who was alive in the woods of North America, Cleek capture her with the hunting net. As the conflict of the family deepens due to the woman, and the eldest daughter releases the woman, the whole situation turns into a crucible of slaughter due to the revenge of the woman. In this movie, the symbolism of each character is important. There is clearly a critical message for patriarchal system, the male violence and its intergenerational transfer. In The Woman, Cleek‚Äôs authority comes from the obedience of the surrounding people. The civilization and edification that Cleek said is a work that eventually destroys the free will of existence and converts it into a state of obeying his orders. It's a resemblance to the colonial regime of whites. In capitalist material civilization, a region with weak aggression becomes a colony in a strong area, but in a civilization divided by gender, women become a male colony. Through The Woman, the director asks us what real ‚Äòcivilization‚Äô is. In the end, after brutally killing her torturers the woman leaves to start the new tribe of women which does not have any male power, with the remaining female characters, which is a new and very satisfying ending.



What scares us in horror movies is that the solid order of this world we are living in is so precarious. The female monsters stand at the boundary between the consciousness and the unconscious, embodying hidden anxiety and fear all over their bodies and interrogating the ‚Äòfather‚Äôs order.‚Äô¬†Emancipating women‚Äôs bodies and sexuality from normativeness will give women a tremendous boost. Conclusion


All Cheerleaders Die(2013)

One of the reasons we are fascinated by horror movies is that we face unethical and immorality that are taboo in society. We can experience the taboos and unethical behaviors defined through social and moral oppressions indirectly through horror movies and feel free. But we don‚Äôt just feel liberated and pleasant. It is possible to understand what is unethical and immoral and what the price comes when they collapse. However, this is only true for those who have a good sense of ethical consciousness. It is impossible that everyone‚Äôs level of ethical consciousness is exemplary, and creators should keep it in their mind. The public learns from the media various times and spaces that they have not actually experienced. As a social being, individuals are socialized by learning the common reality, the so-called mutual subjective reality, shared with other social beings. The reason why learning of mutual subjective reality is possible is because there is an opportunity to objectify subjective experience and share it with others by symbols. Among them, the media lists a series of symbols to select, emphasize, or exclude specific contents. Therefore, it can be understood that the media repeating the reproduction and explanation of a specific way is a political act that reproduces social awareness and value. Media, without worrying about whether it is a problem, supports the cultural norms and beliefs of society and shows the contents that justify and strengthen the current system. Also, most of them show only the values and emotions of the social majority and the way in which events are delivered is also more likely to prefer typical and familiar narrative methods. Like the final girl trope, rape revenge movie formulas, mother characters, and female monsters that appeared repeatedly with deep misogyny in horror movies.



A Girl of A Girl Walks Home Alone at Night(2014)

We still watch male-dominated films from the male-dominated film industry. What should not be forgotten is that the disappearance of women on the screen is not just the area of ‚Äã‚Äã‚Äòentertainment‚Äô. It is a very political survival issue. Since film is a representative social medium that constitutes gender, we should seriously consider this matter. However, in the film industry, where investors, producers, directors, and staff are mostly men, movies are made mainly from a male perspective. The violent and sexual gaze toward women is not only in horror genre but also in other genres. But finally, the female audience began to reject the male gaze on the screen, and movies that rebel against this trend are emerging. As a long-time fan of horror genres, I can see that new horror movies are starting to appear. Theaters are slowly being filled with new types of horror films that have never been seen before. The best example is A Girl Walks Home Alone at Night(2014). This is the first Iranian vampire western movie, and the Iranian vampire girl is the main character. The girl only absorbs male blood, and the action is punishment for their sin. The victims of vampires are men who have forced love to women unilaterally, or who have only regarded them as objects of sexual pleasure. But the movie doesn't just show only her vampire side. The girl, skateboarding around the city and decorating her room with posters and records is an ordinary girl who can easily be seen around us. This work is distinctly different from the horror movies of the past. It is not only a vampire horror film but also expresses the oppressive Islamic culture and the life of women under its system. Like this film, we can make such a beautiful horror movie without repeating discrimination and hate. Some argue that exclusion and violence in the horror genre should be regarded as a cinematic device and genre characteristic for the fun of movies. Some ask why should we discuss ethics in horror movies. However, they cannot be excuses. We demand more in-depth analysis and responsibility from creators beyond simply pointing out gender equality or misogyny. Surely, the misogyny in the horror movie was very thick. It's been the way it has been. But it is changing. This change is being made by us, by the creators. In other words, everything depends on the attitude of the artists and creators, and the genre characteristic is made by them.
